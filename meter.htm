THE STRANGE BUT FUNNY STORY OF MR DIKE

Just this morning, while I was ruminating over my past, I remembered one very funny
but strange thing that happened in my church district some eighteen years ago- how
Mr. Dike learnt a lesson for life. But who might this lesson have come from?
It happened like this.......

Every year, between the month of August and September, we held a sort of a building
project/harvest thanksgiving event. Of course as a tradition, every family was expected
to come prepared for all the festivities which included donating towards the new church
building as well as a harvest for the pastors' welfare. Usually, people danced out
to give their donations or stood up to announced theirs.

Mr. Dike happens to be one of the well-to-dos. But he never liked giving. "All these
pastors and their give give give wahala," he always said to his wife. All her efforts to
change his behaviour fell on deaf ears as he would purposely fall asleep when it's time
to give.

One fateful August, which would mark the fifth and final year for the completion of the
church building, came another "giving time". As usual, Mr Dike went into his 'model'
sleep so as to prevent anybody from expecting him to give. Unfortunately for him,
after the givings were over and while the preacher was giving his sermon, a wooden
support that had broken off its rafter, crashed upon gentle sleeping Mr Dike to
everyone's oblivion. "Yeeeeh! Chineke.Five Hundred and Fifty Thousand Naira",
he screamed rising on his feet.

The entire church was in confusion. Everyone was asking,'what happened?'. Being an
experienced housewife who knew her husband well, Mrs Dike quickly jumped to her
feet, dug into her husband's purse and brought out a cheque of the same amount
being the money they agreed to give from home and then gave it to the church
with a loud "Praise the Lord"
Leaving Mr Dike to writhe in his own pain, the congregation wondered at everything
while most laughed their hearts out upon 'coding' what must have transpired.
No one ever slept during thanksgiving or any fundraising service again.
